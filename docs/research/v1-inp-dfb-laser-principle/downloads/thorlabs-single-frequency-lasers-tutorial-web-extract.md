## Extracted Facts

- Thorlabs compares four single-frequency diode-laser configurations: ECL, DFB, VHG-stabilized and DBR.
- The DFB section describes the grating as internal to the laser diode structure and close to the active region.
- It explains that the corrugated periodic structure acts as a Bragg reflector and selects a single longitudinal mode.
- The tutorial states that DFB lasers often use a phase-shift section to aid mode selection and improve manufacturing yield.
- It gives the DFB Bragg relation in terms of wavelength, effective index and grating period.
- It describes DFB tuning through changes in effective index caused by temperature and current.
- It contrasts DFB and DBR: DFB has the grating along the active/gain region; DBR places Bragg reflectors outside the active region.
- It presents DFB as mode-hop-free over a narrow tuning range and commonly used in telecom and sensing.
- It states that close grating-active-region coupling can limit maximum output power relative to ECL/DBR options.
