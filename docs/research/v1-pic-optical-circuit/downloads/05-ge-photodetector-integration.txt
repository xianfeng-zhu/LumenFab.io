--- Germanium Photodetector Integration ---

Process Flow (Tsuchizawa et al.):
  1. Si waveguide core formation on SOI
  2. Dielectric window opening (SiO2 hard mask)
  3. Selective epitaxial growth of Ge on exposed Si waveguide core
  4. Annealing to reduce threading dislocation density (TDD)
  5. Doping (in situ or ion implantation) to form p-i-n junction
  6. Contact formation (NiSi, W contacts)
  7. Cladding deposition and metallization

Key: Ge is selectively grown only in detector regions where the Si surface is exposed through oxide windows. The Ge sits on top of (or recessed into) the Si waveguide layer.

--- Ge-Fin Photodetector (Nature Photonics 2021) ---
  - Selective growth of Ge body on SOI waveguide
  - SiO2 hard mask deposition and patterning
  - Dry etch of Ge
  - In-situ p-doped Si epitaxy and planarization (one side)
  - Repeat with in-situ n-doped Si epitaxy (opposite side)
  - Forms lateral Si-Ge-Si p-i-n diode without ion implantation
  - Ge fin sandwiched between complementary doped Si layers
  - 3-dB bandwidth up to 265 GHz

--- imec Ge Module ---
  - 100% Ge(Si) RPCVD selective epitaxial growth (RPCVD = Reduced Pressure CVD)
  - 2 implant levels for Ge photodetectors and Ge(Si) EA modulators
  - Waveguide-based Ge-on-Si detectors (light couples evanescently from Si waveguide into Ge)
