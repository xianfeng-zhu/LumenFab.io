--- Metal Heater Placement ---

Conventional design: metal heater (Ti, TiN, Ni, Pt, W) placed on top of the Si waveguide, separated by ~1um SiO2 cladding to prevent optical absorption.

Example cross-section parameters:
  - Si waveguide: 500 nm wide x 220 nm thick (on SOI with 2-3 um BOX)
  - Heater: 2 um wide x 100 nm thick Ti (or 2.5 um x 200 nm Ni)
  - Cladding gap: 1 um (conventional) to 100 nm (advanced PT-symmetric)
  - Typical P_pi = 10-50 mW, switching time = 1-100 us

Large gap (~1um) -> slow thermal response (SiO2 k ~1.4 W/mK). Small gap (<600nm) -> faster but risk of optical loss.

Alternative heater placements:
  - Transparent conductive oxide (ITO) directly on waveguide (gap ~100nm)
  - Doped Si heater within the waveguide itself (higher optical loss)
  - Free-standing/etched structures to remove top cladding and BOX for thermal isolation

In imec iSiPP platform: dedicated metal heater layer. In the Luceda SiFab PDK: drawn on HT layer, heater_offset ~1.0um from center, heater_width ~0.6um, sheet resistance R_sh = 0.5 Ohm/sq. Metal vias connect heater to M1 contact pads.

--- RF Electrode Placement (Traveling-Wave Modulator) ---

Standard TWE modulator cross-section:
  - Substrate: low-resistivity Si (often with grooves for RF isolation)
  - BOX: SiO2, ~2um thick
  - Optical waveguide layer: etched Si rib waveguide with p-n junction at center
  - Doped transition areas: P++ and N++ regions on either side for ohmic contacts
  - ILD: SiO2 cover layer for insulation
  - Electrode structure: coplanar waveguide (GSG or GS) with signal/ground electrodes
  - Conductive vias: W/Al vias connecting metal electrodes to doped Si regions

Design targets: 50 Ohm characteristic impedance, velocity matching between RF (n_microwave ~4-5) and optical (n_group ~3.5-4.5) modes.
